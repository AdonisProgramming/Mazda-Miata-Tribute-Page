Mazda Miata

The Joy of Driving Returns

A first generation Miata featured on Workaholics deservingly

  • 1976: Motor Trend Magazine journalist and Japanese car enthusiast Bob Hall met Kenichi Yamamoto and Gai Arai, the head of Research and Development at Mazda. They asked what kind of car Bob would like to see...

    • "I babbled on how the simple, bugs-in-the-teeth, wind-in-the-hair, classically-British sports car doesn't exist any more. I told Mr. Yamamoto that somebody should build one. A inexpensive roadster."

    - Bob Hall

  • 1982: 6 years later, Yamamoto becomes the chairman of Mazda and give Bob the go ahead to further research the roadster project
  • 1983: Bob and his California research team's concept was approved but they still faced more trials ahead as they would have to prove their concept was best aganist two other Tokyo design teams
  • Bob's team designed a front engine RWD (Rear wheel drive) concept and the two Tokyo teams went with a mid-engine (engine is where the trunk should be) RWD concept and a front-engine FWD (Front wheel drive) concept, respectively


    • Front engine with front wheel drive concept, Tokyo Design Team Concept

    Mid engine with rear wheel drive concept, Tokyo Design Team Concept

    Front engine with rear wheel drive concept, Bob's California Team Concept

    Bob's concept had one major difference it could be topless



  • 1986: Bob's concept was selected and recieved final approval for production.
  • Mazda felt that the front engine and rear wheel drive concept embodied the essence of the classic British roadster and could be made lightweight, quick-turning, reliable, and affordable. Also it was a convertible, which is cool.
  • 1989: The Mazda Miata was unvealed at the Chicago Auto Show nervously as the idea of a two seater convertible was daring considering the auto market was comprised of safe economical high mile per gallon sedans

    • A white Miata (shown) along with a red and blue version were featured at the 1989 Chicago Auto Show

  • 1989 - 1997: The first generation of the Miata scored leaps and bounds amoung reviewers and consumers. It was Automobile Magazine's 1990 Automobile of the Year and Car and Driver's 10 Best list from 1990 to 1992 along with numerous awards and boostful sales numbers.
  • 1999-Current: The Miata has seen 4 different generations: the NA, NB, NC, and the current ND. Mazda has sold over 1 million units and the Japanese created classic British convertible sports car has been renowed as a modern classic.



  • Renowned Top Gear host Jeremy Clarkson said it best when reviewing what the best British Sports Car is...

    • "The MX-5 (Miata) kicked off the sports car Renaissance and ever since it came along in 1989 everyone has been trying to beat it...but you can't beat the orignal recipe, a recipe written more than 60 years ago.
    Keep it simple."

    - Jeremy Clarkson
